About me

I am an engineer raised in western Iowa with my BSME from Iowa State University. I have professional experience in aerospace and mechanical design industries, with a focus in manufacturing, rapid prototyping, and data analysis.

Over the past 5 years, I have gained valuable experience as a mechanical engineer through a Boeing sponsored project course called Make2Innovate and during my time at the Ames National Laboratory where I gained skills related to data analysis. I got accustomed to working in multi disciplinary teams and operating under strict deadlines.

Projects

Reverse Engineering Manure Spreader

  • Problem: Manufacturer faced high overhead and supply chain volatility due to reliance on European-sourced assemblies. Increased tariffs and large ordering requirements led to financial inefficiency and long lead times.
  • Role: Was responsible for the technical documentation and cost-benefit analysis required to transition production to domestic suppliers. Aided in CAD modeling of the frame and manufacturing feasibility.
  • Tools and methods: SolidWorks, FEA, DFMEA, Value Stream Mapping, Function Flow Block Diagrams
  • Process: Performed teardown of the frame assembly to document critical tolerances, material specifications, and interface geometries.
  • Result: Produced a full technical data package including 3D models and drawings for the assembly housing. Developed a sourcing strategy that reduced lead times.

FSAE Vehicle Suspension Optimization

  • Problem: Optimize the suspension system of the Formula SAE CR-28 vehicle against real world data.
  • Role: Used SolidWorks to determine physical parameters like the distance from the roll axis to the center of gravity. Analyzed front and rear wing configurations to estimate cornering efficiency and ensure the aero kit's mass and downforce.
  • Tools: MATLAB, AIM Race Studio, SolidWorks
  • Process: Used SolidWorks to calculate vehicle dimensions. Compared simulated lateral acceleration against real-world data captured via an AIM module during controlled skid pad testing.
  • Result: Achieved a simulation accuracy within 5.8%, and predicted a 1.65g lateral acceleration compared to 1.56g observed in physical testing.

Make2Innovate — CyLaunch

  • Problem: Design and build a rocket to compete in the NASA Student Launch Challenge.
  • Role: Oversaw simulation operations of rocket (CR4IG) using Star CCM+, and facilitated in the design of the air brake assembly.
  • Tools and methods: Star CCM+, SolidWorks, Arduino, C++, Rapid Prototyping
  • Process: Developed rocket models in SolidWorks and performed Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations in Star-CCM+ to analyze aerodynamic stability and pressure distributions.
  • Result: Disqualified in the 2nd round of NASA competition due to parachute actuation issues causing safety concerns.
